Featured project

A pan-Arctic thaw-slump survey, built end to end by one scientist

RTSmapping v2 detects retrogressive thaw slumps — the most dramatic form of abrupt permafrost thaw — in 3 m PlanetScope imagery. One scientist designed the dataset, trained and audited the models, benchmarked them against 6.7B-parameter foundation encoders, and deployed a 32-GPU fleet that swept the southern Arctic — delivering an open map of 10,984 active thaw slumps.

01 · Science

Referee-proof by design

Leakage-free spatial splits, three-seed ensembles, object metrics corrected to the minimum mapping unit, and a full pre-launch audit. Benchmarked head-to-head against SAM2 and satellite foundation models up to 6.7B parameters — the purpose-built 30M-parameter UNet++ wins at a fraction of the compute.

PR-AUC — UNet++ 0.92 · ViT-L 0.92 · 7B frozen 0.76 · SAM2 0.56

02 · Engineering

Planetary-scale inference

An atomic shard-claim queue on cloud storage drove a self-healing fleet of 32 L4 GPUs through 41,567,572 tiles with exactly-once reconciliation — every tile accounted for. Docker-reproducible end to end, with a 338-test suite guarding the pipeline.

2,079 shards · 0 tiles lost · 338 tests green

03 · Product

From pixels to open maps

Model output on 2025 imagery — Northwest Territories, Canada

10,984 thaw-slump polygons across 238 km², released as open GeoPackage and cloud-optimized rasters. Builds on the open ARTS benchmark (Scientific Data) and deep-learning mapping in RSE.
